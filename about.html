PUZZLE BOX

Puzzle Box is a box with an LCD screen that displays the questions and a keypad to enter the answers. I began by designing 3 questions, each focused on a different aspect of information security techniques.

1. SIMPLE ENCODING
Q1: LOOK AROUND YOU, BUT NOT TOO FAR. WHAT DO YOU SEE?
Obviously, this question doesn’t mean to look around your environment. Look at the bottom right corner in the LCD screen, you can notice something blinking like this:  
HINT1.1: ITS NOT MORSE CODE, SMARTY, BUT YOURE CLOSE
If you write them down, you will notice 4 characters:
----. ..--. -.... -.--.
At first glance, you might think it’s Morse code, but once you decode it—using any online decoder—, you will notice some unusual characters: ĤEÐ6(
So, obiously it's not Morse code, what else it could be?
HINT1.2: PAY ATTENTION TO THE CHARACTER BIT LENGTH
This tells you exactly what type of encoding this method uses. In Morse code, we notice that the character bit length is not fixed and varies depending on the letter. However, the characters you obtained have a fixed character bit length of 5. If you Google it, you will find an older encoding with a 5-bit length, which is called Baudot Code
The Baudot Code was the most common teleprinter code before ASCII. The next step is easy: use the Baudot Code table—from Wiki—to decode the characters.
SHOW ANSWER
DBAC

GREAT! YOU LEARNED A NEW THING TODAY

2. CRYPTOGRAPHY
Q2: XGAP CFC QKA ITTMSHYFSQU NHSBDOGA TSCSU?
Don't panic! Yes, it's in English, but it's encrypted. You need to decrypt the question first before answering it. If you know a thing or two about cryptography, you'll understand that you need a secret key to encrypt/decrypt a message. But where can you find this key?
HINT2.1: DONT TELL ME YOU FORGOT YOUR FIRST ANSWER
This means the first answer is the secret key: DBAC
Ok, you've got the secret key. What's next? Yes, of course, you need to figure out the cipher.
HINT2.2: YOU NEED TO PLAY THIS GAME FAIRLY TO WIN
My fav hint :) So if you know more than a thing or two about cryptography, and you're familiar with common symmetric key ciphers, this hint says it all.
Yes, it's the PlayFair Cipher
The PlayFair cipher was the first practical digraph substitution cipher. To learn more about it, check this link out.
Using the secret key, you first fill the table with it, followed by the rest of the alphabet. Now you can substitute the shifted letters—or simply use an online decoder. After decrypting the entire question, you will get this:
WHEN DID THE CORONAVIRUS PANDEMIC START?
SHOW ANSWER
2019

YOU TOOK ALL THIS TIME TO RECALL WHEN COVID-19 START? :)

3. STEGANOGRAPHY
Q3: CAN YOU TELL Me WHEN IS YOUR BIRTH YeAR?
Try to enter your birth year ;)
HINT3.1: HOW AM I SUPPOSED TO KNOW YOUR BIRTH YEAR
Exactly! I'm just a program, not Akinator. So, the question itself might seem meaningless, but there is something hidden within it!
HINT3.2: THE Es ARE TRYNA TELL YOU SOMETHING
If you strip all the 'E's from the question, you will get: EeEe
But when you try to enter it, you’ll notice there is no 'E' key on the keypad :( However, there is one thing we can do: we can try using its binary representation!
E -> 1
e -> 0
SHOW ANSWER
1010
